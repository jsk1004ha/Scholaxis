Core Abstract

We propose a novel framework for Quantum-Enhanced Deep Learning (QEDL) that leverages high-dimensional entanglement to optimize weight distributions in neural architectures. Current classical limitations in gradient descent for trillion-parameter models often encounter "barren plateaus"—a problem we mitigate using the geometric properties of Hilbert space.

Our results demonstrate a 40% reduction in training latency and a significant increase in inference precision for non-linear time-series prediction. This paper details the structural isomorphism between transformer attention mechanisms and photonic interference patterns.
